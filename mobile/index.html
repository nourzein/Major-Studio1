The Rich History of Color in Europe

After the Fall of Rome in 450 CE, Europe plunged into the Dark Ages, experiencing four centuries of cultural and artistic stagnation where classical painting and color development were halted. However, a glorious resurrection happened around the 11th century.

This is a color analysis of the MET's European Paintings Collection by artistic movement (from 1200-1900).
Explore each art movement seperately to see the evolution of color usage.

Analysis Takeaways

  • Red and reddish shades of Brown were among the first colors used in Europe (brought from Asia through trade).
  • Red continued to remain the most dominant color used throughout the centuries along with Orange . It was cheap and widely accessible.
  • During the Renaissance period, oil painting was established allowing artists more innovation in painting techniques, and as a consequence, subject matter. We see first signs of Yellow and Green .
  • Periods with nature-based paintings (ex: Rococo and Romanticism) show predominant use of Green (along with red).
  • Although there were signs of blue early on, Blue only became popular in the 18th century because before then it was very expensive.
